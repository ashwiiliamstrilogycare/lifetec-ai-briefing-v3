Why it worked · our approach

Problem first. People first. Small steps.

We started with the pain.

Not “where can we put AI?” — “what's wasting our people's time?” Then we fixed that.

We augmented, not replaced.

AI does the grunt work and drafts. People judge, decide and sign. Always.

We proved it, then grew.

One job at a time, measured before the next. No big-bang, no big risk.

What's possible for LifeTec

Imagine your clinicians spending their time on people — not paperwork.

The assessment still needs your OT. The communication still needs your speech pathologist. But the writing-up, the funding forms, the chasing — handled. That's how ~20% becomes 60%: not more hours, less admin.

What's possible · four places to start

Built around what your OTs and speech pathologists already do.

AT assessments · Functional capacity

Reports & NDIS requests, written for you

The clinician does the assessment; the AI turns the notes into the formal AT report and funding request — clinical justification built in, ready to review and sign.

↳ Hours of writing back, on every client.
NDIS · My Aged Care · CHSP · Support at Home

The right funding pathway, every time

It matches each client to the correct scheme and pre-fills the application against that program's rules — so requests land first time.

↳ Fewer knock-backs, faster approvals.
Intake · Triage · Scheduling

Clients arrive ready

First contact is captured, triaged and booked, and the clinician gets a clean pre-visit summary before they walk in the door.

↳ Visit time on the person, not the form.
Home modifications · AAC trials

Scopes, quotes & trial plans in minutes

From site notes to a costed, compliant minor-home-mod scope — or an equipment trial plan matched to the client's needs and the catalogue.

↳ Less back-office, more jobs done.

How you'd start · and why you're well placed

At around twenty people, you can move fast.

A small team is the advantage — less to change, quicker to see the win. You don't need a strategy. You need one job.

Step one

Pick one painful job

The repetitive, time-eating task your team dreads. One is enough to prove the value.

Step two
